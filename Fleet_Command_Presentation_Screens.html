Screen 01
Dashboard — Operations Tab · General View

Mental model: the user expects a single pane of glass for fleet status at a glance. They can scan fleet health, KPIs, alerts, and map to understand “what’s happening now.” Actions: switch date range, open alerts, drill into map markers, navigate to vehicles or tasks. Decisions: whether to intervene (e.g., address alerts), allocate resources, or schedule maintenance based on posture and SLA indicators.

Screen 02
Dashboard — Operations Tab · Calendar

Mental model: the user expects to control the time window for all operations data. They can pick a date range to answer “what happened in this period?” Actions: select dates, change range presets (e.g., last 7 days), apply filters. Decisions: which time slice to analyze, whether to compare periods, and how to scope reports or investigations.

Screen 03
Dashboard — Operations Tab · Search Feature

Mental model: the user expects quick access to any vehicle, driver, or entity by typing. They can jump directly to a specific asset instead of browsing. Actions: type a query, select a result, open an entity detail. Decisions: which asset to inspect first, and whether to act immediately from search results or navigate deeper.

Screen 04
Dashboard — Operations Tab · Metrics Tooltip

Mental model: the user expects to trust the numbers by understanding how they’re calculated. The tooltip answers “why this score?” and “what’s included?” Actions: hover or click to reveal methodology. Decisions: whether to rely on the metric, adjust operations based on it, or flag methodology concerns to stakeholders.

Screen 05
Dashboard — Cost & Performance Tab

Mental model: the user expects to see spend and efficiency in one place—“how much are we spending and how effective are we?” They can compare cost vs. performance across vehicles or time. Actions: filter by segment, drill into cost drivers, export for reporting. Decisions: where to cut costs, which assets underperform, and how to reallocate budget based on ROI or utilization.

Screen 06
Dashboard — Analysis Tab

Mental model: the user expects to explore trends, patterns, and insights—“what’s changing over time and why?” They can slice data by dimension and compare metrics. Actions: change date range, switch dimensions, add/remove metrics, export charts. Decisions: which trends matter, whether to escalate issues, and what strategic changes to propose based on evidence.

Section 07
Research & Inspiration

Four fleet management platforms were studied before any design decisions were made: myGEOTAB, Verizon Reveal+, Fleetio, and Samsara / Motive. From myGEOTAB, the tab-based dashboard structure and unified tracking surface were carried forward; from Fleetio, the card-based KPI layout and clean table density.

Samsara and Motive established the industry-standard traffic-light status semantics (green / amber / red) and the anomaly-first approach - surfacing what needs attention rather than displaying all data equally. Verizon Reveal+ served as the anti-pattern: a wall of equal-weight bar charts in competing colors, demonstrating data without hierarchy.

What was deliberately left behind across all competitors was the generic light-mode enterprise aesthetic, fragmented module structures across separate apps, and the passive "here is your data" tone that shifts resolution burden to the operator.

The $27B fleet management market is dominated by established platforms that are strong on data collection but weak on decision clarity. Fleet Command OS is positioned in that gap - between surfacing information and resolving it.

Carried Forward
Tab-based navigation, KPI cards, unified tracking surface.
Core UX Constraint
Anomaly-first hierarchy using consistent traffic-light status semantics.
Anti-Pattern
Equal visual weight across every chart, without clear decision priority.
Deliberately Avoided
Fragmented modules, generic enterprise light-mode, passive reporting tone.

Design System v1.2
